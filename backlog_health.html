How Backlog Health Is Calculated

What We Look At

  • Included: Story tickets in the **active Product Backlog** (those slated for future sprints AND those currently unassigned but actively being groomed)
  • Counted as “Healthy”: Tickets in statuses like Ready for Development or To Do
  • Not Counted as Healthy: Tickets in New, Grooming, or Backlog statuses
  • Excluded: Tickets that are Done, Canceled, or in active (current/past) sprints

Backlog Health Calculation

Backlog Health reflects the percentage of story tickets in the **active Product Backlog** (which includes those slated for future sprints and those that are currently unassigned but actively being groomed) that are in a “Ready for Development” or “To Do” status. These are considered healthy and immediately plannable.

Tickets in statuses like “New”, “Grooming”, or “Backlog” are not counted as healthy. However, we display all statuses here to give complete transparency into what’s in the backlog.

What the Percentages Mean

  • ✅ Green: 80% or more ready
  • 🟡 Yellow: 50–79% ready
  • 🔴 Red: Less than 50% ready

Industry References

Scrum.org, SAFe, and Atlassian recommend readiness buffer management based on statuses, not velocity.
